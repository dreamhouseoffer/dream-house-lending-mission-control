TRAINING CONTENT:
This training covers how to think through active mortgage files when lender interpretation, VOE details, income calculations, AUS findings, reserves, and manual underwriting become blockers.

Core lessons:
1. Lender matrix risk: Do not blindly trust a matrix if underwriters may interpret it differently. If a lender denies or interprets the file differently than expected, quickly identify another lender/bank that can take the file and communicate timeline resets clearly.
2. Timeline reset when moving lenders: Moving a file is not instant. Disclosure, lock, review, waiting periods, and lender turn times can restart. Explain to agents/sellers: “We found a solution, but need time because the file must move through new lender requirements.”
3. VOE completeness: On Verification of Employment, make sure item 12A/current gross pay is fully completed. For hourly employees, confirm the hourly rate. Confirm the “through date” for year-to-date income so income is calculated correctly.
4. Rehire history: If borrower left and returned to a long-time employer, get the original hire date and rehire date. This explains low/interrupted income years and helps underwriter understand continuity.
5. Email confirmation is usable support: If employer does not want to revise the VOE again, ask them to confirm missing items by email. Save that email as PDF to the file/Dropbox.
6. Variable income review: For overtime, commissions, bonus, or inconsistent earnings, compare year-to-date, prior year, and job history. Do not include a year that clearly distorts income without understanding why.
7. School/seasonal employee pay: For school/food service or non-12-month employees, verify whether they are paid over 10, 11, or 12 months. Convert annual/base pay correctly into monthly qualifying income.
8. Arive/MCM consistency: After changing income, sales price, down payment, assets, liabilities, or payoffs, update both Arive and MCM so AUS and scenario numbers match.
9. Payoff vs omit debts: “Paid off at closing” means the debt is paid through the transaction and may help ratios. “Omitted” means the debt is removed for another acceptable reason. Do not confuse the two.
10. AUS/reserve testing: If DU gives Refer instead of Approve/Eligible, test assets/reserves and ratios. But if score/profile still does not work, explain that reserves alone may not overcome the risk.
11. Manual underwriting: When AUS refers, tighter front-end/back-end DTI limits and compensating factor requirements matter. Know when the file has enough reserves/residual income/payment shock support and when it does not.
12. Borrower/client calls: For sensitive cost/rate/points questions, call the borrower when a simple text will create confusion. Use MCM to walk through loan amount, rate, points, closing costs, escrows, prepaid items, and lender fees.

KEY RULES / TAKEAWAYS:
- VOE must show current pay structure, hourly rate if hourly, YTD through date, and hire/rehire history when applicable.
- Save employer email confirmations as PDF when they clarify missing VOE details.
- Recalculate income conservatively and consistently across MCM and Arive.
- For school or seasonal employees, verify whether pay is 10, 11, or 12 months before using monthly income.
- If AUS gives Refer, review reserves, ratios, and compensating factors before assuming the file works.
- Moving lenders can save a file but resets important timelines. Communicate that early.
- Cost/points/rate confusion should be handled by phone with a clear MCM breakdown.
